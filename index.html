It cannot be denied that employment is important when it comes to discussing a country's economic standing as employment dictates the societal and wealth growth of a country.

Hence, as we discuss the state of employment trends in Malaysia during the 2000's and 2010's we are able to see how this Southeast Asian nation with it's diverse economy evolve over time as different sectors deal with different challenges.

Within this window of time marks substantial growth as Malaysia is home to many industries dealing with export and evergrowing population, there exist a demand for manpower leading to job opportunities which will be discussed.

Unemployment rate of Malaysian States (2005 to 2020)





The unemployment rate for the Malaysian states remain fairly constant with occasional higher rates in North-West Malaysia.

The unemployment rate remains high throughout the years in Sabah due to relatively underdeveloped infrastructure and many remote locations within its state.

Employers Contribution By Sector and State (2015 to 2017)









We observe the breakdown of the sectors of which employees work in within each sector for each state.

Generally, Wholesale & Retail and Manufacturing has generally more employees due to the daily operations which reguire a large workforce this would encompass tasks that relate to goods production and managing inventory, sales and customer service.

Additionally, there always exist consumer demand for the output of this sectors.

Employers Contribution State and Company Size (2016 to 2018)














The darkness of the colour saturation represent the total number of employees within companies of a specific type of size whether that be Small and Medium-sized Enterprises (SME) and Large enterprises.

That being said the darker and larger the circle the higher the total number of employees within a state.

Throughout the years Kuala Lumpur, Selangor, Penang and Johor have the highest number of employees of all company size.

Employment Status by Field of Study (2013 to 2016)




Social science, business, and law sectors may have an increase in both employed and unemployed individuals deal to a general increase in graduates in these fields.



The Manufacturing sector has seen a rise in employment and a decline in unemployment with growing global demand for manufactured products.

Employment by Occupation Category and Gender (2010 to 2016)




It is easily observed the different occupation categories and the disparity of male and female demographic presence in each field.

Overall, we can see that General workers, Agriculture, Machine Operators and Managerial positions have a negligible disparity between genders in these fields.

Skills Needed by Job Category (Public Consensus in 2016)




This represents the public perception through a survey done in 2016 regarding the importance of a skill needed for a job category.

The y-scale is the percentage of the sample population in Malaysia that believe a skill is important for a job category.

Overall, Managers is perceived to require the most skills whereas Agriculture is perceived to need the least amount of skills.
